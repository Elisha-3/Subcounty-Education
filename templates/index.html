SCDE Department
SCDE Overview

The Sub-County Director of Education (SCDE) is responsible for overseeing the management and coordination of education services in the sub-county. The SCDE ensures that educational policies are implemented effectively, teachers are well-trained, and schools maintain high standards.

Key Responsibilities
  • Implementing education policies from the Ministry of Education.
  • Managing teacher deployment in public schools within the sub-county.
  • Supervising school administration and ensuring compliance with national standards.
  • Coordinating educational programs and projects in the sub-county.
  • Managing school infrastructure development projects.
SCDE Initiatives

The SCDE is actively involved in several initiatives aimed at improving the quality of education in the sub-county, including:

  • Infrastructure upgrades for primary and secondary schools.
  • Teacher capacity-building programs and training workshops.
  • Monitoring and evaluation of school performance and student progression.

SCQASO Department
Sub-County Quality Assurance and Standards Officer (SCQASO)

Ensuring education quality and standards are upheld in Nyamira South Sub-County.

SCQASO Overview

The Sub-County Quality Assurance and Standards Officer (SCQASO) is tasked with maintaining and improving the quality of education in the sub-county. The SCQASO monitors and evaluates schools to ensure they comply with educational standards.

Key Responsibilities
  • Conducting regular inspections of schools to ensure compliance with quality standards.
  • Advising school management on best practices for improving education quality.
  • Evaluating teaching methods and learning outcomes in schools.
  • Ensuring adherence to safety and health regulations in schools.
  • Reporting and documenting school performance and challenges to the Ministry of Education.
SCQASO Initiatives

The SCQASO is involved in initiatives aimed at raising educational standards, such as:

  • Periodic teacher evaluations and training sessions.
  • School improvement programs and performance monitoring.
  • Ensuring implementation of the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) standards.

Audit Department

Audit Department

The Audit Department of the Sub County Education Office is responsible for ensuring transparency, accountability, and compliance with financial and operational procedures across all educational institutions in the sub-county. Our mandate is to conduct thorough audits of financial records, resource utilization, and administrative processes to safeguard public funds and improve efficiency. By offering objective evaluations and recommendations, we aim to strengthen governance, minimize risks, and promote responsible stewardship of resources in the education sector.

Financial Audits Compliance Audits
We conduct thorough reviews of school financial records to ensure accuracy, transparency, and compliance with budgeting and financial regulations.
We evaluate the effectiveness of internal controls to safeguard institutional resources, identify risks, and recommend improvements to prevent fraud and misuse of funds.
We assess how schools use their resources, including funds, materials, and equipment, to promote efficiency and accountability.
We verify that institutions are following government regulations, policies, and procedures in financial management, procurement, and other administrative functions.
We offer workshops and training for school administrators and finance staff to help them better understand financial management, compliance, and audit processes.

Clerical/Records Department
Clerical/Records Department

Efficient record management for the Nyamira South Sub-County Education Office.

Overview

The Clerical/Records Department is responsible for handling all documentation and records for the Nyamira South Sub-County Education Office. The department ensures that records are well-organized, easily accessible, and securely stored in both physical and digital formats.

Key Responsibilities
  • Maintaining accurate and up-to-date records of schools, students, and staff in the sub-county.
  • Archiving important documents, including school reports, examination results, and government policies.
  • Issuing and receiving official documents, such as certificates and reports, from schools and other educational institutions.
  • Processing student admissions, transfers, and other records-related requests.
  • Ensuring secure storage and handling of confidential information.
Services Provided

The Clerical/Records Department offers the following services to the public and education institutions:

  • Retrieval of archived school records and reports.
  • Verification of student and staff documentation for compliance purposes.
  • Assistance with records management for schools within the sub-county.
  • Guidance on document submission for new schools and institutions.

Digital Records Management

The department is also in the process of digitizing its records for improved efficiency. This will allow schools, parents, and other stakeholders to access important information online in a secure and timely manner.
